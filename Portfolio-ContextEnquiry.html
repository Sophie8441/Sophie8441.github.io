Exploring Persuading Behavior Change intervention in personal dietary behaviors :

A Diary Study Approach Applied in the Context of Home-based Cardiac Rehabilitation to Maintain Sustained Healthy Dietary Habits in Everyday Diet Practices

Patients living with cardiovascular disease experience difficulty in maintaining healthy dietary behaviors. Engaging patients in meaningful diet activities encourages them to be in charge of their own healthy dietary behaviors. Considering personal health reasons and factors, the home-based CR program in the telemedicine context shows potential in providing tailored health dietary solutions. Therefore, this explorative study focuses on aspects of behavior change on the interplay between CR patients and their everyday life, particularly in the home environment. By using persuading behavior change interventions the exploration engages 6 participants in a 2-week in-context diary study through goal-directed, self-monitoring, and self-determination in their daily diet practices. All participants have motivations for maintaining sustained healthy dietary habits. The contextualized findings in understanding the individualized diet scenarios can be used as source of inspiration and as evidence for further design work in applying persuasive health interventions in the home-based CR context.

This project focused on design research stage in exploring the contextual scenario to scope the design possibility, and the findings generated were used in my final master project.

Mapping of requirements from literature understanding

 

Context Exploration  

The results from literature review and the discussion with the expert demonstrated that a behavior monitoring method can provide a good opportunity for behavior change. The decision was made to focus on developing a consistent diet experience for CR patients to take part in home-based CR on a regular basis. I then reflected on how to connect and make sense of the interfaces, activities, and personal data gathered through the experience with healthcare providers’ touchpoints (specialized dieticians). This resulted in the interpretation of the relevant elements in this iteration, which are goal identification, diet planning, diet monitoring, and regular consulting.

The focused scenario in connecting the key elements

 

A diary approach to inquest potential scenario (UX)   

The use of diary approach is to create an engaging diet activity encoded with the identified key elements in the target context. A two-week diary study with six participants was conducted to assess the potential role of these elements and its influence on behavior change in the exact scenario.

The process of the exploratory study

The study’s goal is to investigate the feasibility of implementing the proposed persuading behavior change protocol in the context of routine activity at home while also investigating the scope for technological support by involving participants in situations where they have a motivated target diet goal to follow. The study is built on the need for more in-depth and contextual knowledge to explore and understand the complexity and adaptation in designing technologies with the goal of having a long-term impact on everyday diet-related practices. The anticipated outcomes seek to unearth issues related to user experiences and personal values that may emerge from self-reflection practices. 

Weeky report (collected daily data from the participant)    that was sent to individual participant for the longer term reflection

 

Reflection 

The used of two-weeks diary study as an user test 

The desire to investigate the dynamics of behavioral change within a population with a clear interest in making dietary changes for health reasons was one of the driving forces behind our study of CR patients as a behavioral change domain. Because the altered behaviors can clearly benefit this target group (User & Society). As an alternative, a 2-week diary study was conducted to learn more about how users who must adhere to specific tracking patterns think and feel (Design & Research).

Support behavior change by raising awareness 

I observed how involved participants use a goal-directed and reflection-persuaded approach to place themselves in a continuum self-monitoring environment to determine how much dietary change is required. These methods support the participants' eating behaviors by increasing their awareness and empowering them further through reflection.

Implications for home-based CR 

The study’s findings revealed that food intake measurements are critical in tracking dietary data, but a measurable approach that can be integrated into the user’s diet experience should be considered for future research.

Design Challenge : the informed step 

These contextualized findings from this exploratory study assist me in better scoping feasible and concrete understanding of designing implementations from potential user's point of view. I therefore deconstructure the scenario where includes the elements recognized from the participants.

User experience mapping in the target scenario

From a patient-centered standpoint, the core elements are denoted by green circles. Diet goal identification, behavior monitoring, dieting (plating and eating), and reflections on target (goal) using the methods employed are all part of this. The purple squares are used to delineate the points of contact with healthcare providers (the specialized dietician). Figures in the gray background represent contextual elements for personalized considerations. Individualized motivation, self-monitoring tools, and context are among these elements. The blue circles represent the personalized diet context, which is heavily influenced by personalized, established habits and values. Finally, the pink circles emphasize the self-reflection circle, in which individuals demonstrate their awareness, mindfulness, and performance. Individuals' healthy eating patterns are influenced by these factors, which in turn influence their actions in maintaining sustained dietary behaviors.
