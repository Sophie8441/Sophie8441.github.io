Vision on Design 

Modern life is increasingly fast-paced, which can lead to an individual's stress and difficulty with life choices. This situation, therefore, emphasizes the importance of a reflective mindset to an individual in relation to one's behaviors and lifestyle toward a healthy and desired way (Li, Dey & Forlizzi, 2010).

Ghajargar et al. (2018) identify Reflection as an ongoing activity that should be continuously supported rather than a certain task or problem to be solved, which can be deemed as a challenge for designers designing tools for supporting a continuous activity. As such, by reassessing and analyzing individualized experiences and relevant contextual information people can then be motivated to be in charge of the target behaviors/lifestyle (MÃ¼ller et al., 2012) and can further be supported to achieve self-empowerment in being able to make informed decisions through a reflective attitude (Garcia et al, 2014).

In the sense that reflective thinking can act as a rational approach to nudge individuals toward desired behaviors, Baumer et al. (2014) also considered reflection a boost to traditional and persuasive ways of behavior change, particularly in terms of sustainable behaviors.

In addition, Ghajargar et al. (2018) introduced make me think relationships, in which they reconstruct the relationship between the tool (design artifact) and the other elements in the system. Particularly, They recognized these kinds of tools as mediums to make the user think and reflect on an undertaken activity in order to accomplish a functional goal. In that regard, it is the designer's job to recognize those relationships between the tool for reflection and other elements in the system in order to affect the user for healthier sustained behaviors/lifestyles in their everyday life.

Devlopment in Expertise Areas 

User and Society (U&S) is a fundamental area in design, which allows designers to understand and explore the users' needs and values, as well as the relationship between the environment. In this regard, I value qualitative methods during different phases of the design study as these methods enable me to approach the user in a certain context. By recognizing and mapping the relevant elements in the target scenarios, I am able to explore and inquire about concrete and adapted questions throughout the study phases. In particular, I am enthusiastic to explore the use of different probes (e.g. culture probes, participatory design, and technology probes) to be able to fit into different design stages. I believe that such qualitative methodology can act as a very important role to capture the reflections, the needs, the values, the understanding, and the experiences of the users and the stakeholders more in-depth. And this could be much help when it comes to developing a more thorough understanding from the user's/stakeholders perspective. In order to bring the design concepts to a more grounded level, I like employing user evaluation techniques in the iterative design process. On one hand, the evaluation can be used to gain and generate new knowledge (research) and on the other hand to understand the user's contextualized thoughts and experiences (design).

Creativity & Aesthetics (C&A) is crucial to me as a designer. I believe this area can drive the design to create sustainable values for the user and society, as well as help users to attain their personalized goals in their presumed target scenarios. I consider it important to bring a sense of attachment to the design for users, and C&A can play a key role in this. The aesthetic forms of design can further act as a comfortable conversation and the medium to display one's identity and shape the personal style through physical products or digital media. A designed artifact with the characteristics of aesthetics and creativity can shape one's mind, and influence one's behaviors, activities, and way of thinking (Malafouris, 2008; Salomon, 1993; Turkle, 2011).

In my design work, I do a lot of visual work, such as making 2D & 3D sketches, visuals, mockups, and even interactive prototypes throughout the iterative design and development process. This helps me communicate my ideas to clients and other stakeholders more efficiently. I realize that sketching skills would be essential when there's an obvious gap between stakeholders with different backgrounds. A clear way of visualization is able to bring people to the same understanding page. I believe that creativity (innovation) has to stay grounded and be made applicable in the iterative development process. My approach as such is intended to adopt an explorative mindset to communicate the creative concept with the users and the stakeholders. Throughout discussion and analysis, the conceptual ideas can thus be brought to a more practical and reliable level with perspectives from different backgrounds. And from a designer's perspective, the aesthetic aspects of design are able to communicate the designer's underlying conceptual model in a clear and consistent manner.

As a designer, I believe U&S allows me to think and to realize from different perspectives in terms of the needs and the values of the individual and its environmental implications. The contextual experiences concern more intrinsic and comfortable conversation, which I believe C&A can bring more knowledge to me in this sense. Therefore, U&S and C&A can be described as two of the core skills and abilities that I identify myself to equip at this stage considering my personality and background.
