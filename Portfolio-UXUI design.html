UX theory & practice

A sustainable investment digital application

The project challenge is provided by a client, which is a leading financial services company in Norway. The focus is on the implementation of conversational AI in the investment field. The aim is to trigger young adults to start investing. In the design and research process, user research is developed to draw the potential user journey and the final layout is a UI.

Time frame: 04.2021 (one week project)
Category: UX Theories & Practice, UI, Sustainable design
Development: User and Society, Creativity and Aesthetics
Course: UX theory & practices

Design Process  

Given the current context of the pandemic, the competition already joined this market by targeting these young people to show them they could start investing. Thatâ€™s where the client also want to commit to. By offering young adults proper channels, the focus group are able to convince themselves in doing investment.

Based on our own understanding and experiences of using banking relevant apps (1st person perspective), there are 3 aspects that exist in the general user experiences: overwhelming information, hard to understand, and risky considerations. To understand more in depth about how people perceive using banking apps, we first start with an online survey with responses from 30 participants. 63.3% (19 out of 30) of participants showed that they had never invested before, in which 84.2% (16 out of 19) presented that it was because they had no idea about where and how to start.

The above findings showed that the importance of investment can be aligned with personal values. This can be facilitated with the personal learning process and thus have a long-term sustainable impact.

UX Manifesto (Interpretation from UX) 

The elective course "UX theory & practice" concluded with the creation of a manifesto containing concrete declarations regarding the meaning of User Experience (UX). This was built upon by interpreting the relevant UX theories and also the practices of the challenge provided by the industry.

In this Manifesto, we construct the aspects regarding the importance of the use of persona within the UX design process. We are convinced that one of the core aspects of User Experience (UX) is Persona, considering that it is a representation of the user group targeted. The persona is a widely used tool by designers, as a way to keep the user's values and needs in mind, in order to avoid stereotypical thoughts and understanding (User & Society). Therefore, we analyze the importance that the persona has within the UX by highlighting four aspects present in its composition. These explored dimensions: empathy, behavior, the context, as well as the decision making, are related to the user and the (in)direct involvement of the user with the design and respectively with the design. Eventually, we conclude by Persona is the vehicle to drive UX when designing for User Experience.
