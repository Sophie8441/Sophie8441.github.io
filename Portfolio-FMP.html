A tangible deployment to support personal fruit-intake habits in the home-based cardiac rehabilitation context.

This is collaborated project with Maxima Medisch Centrum in the Netherlands in an e-health domain. The final deliverable of the project is an ioT system that includes a tangible design and the connected digital UI design. The goals are to involve e-health technology to improve the current-used digital platform for being seamlessly integrated with existing workflows and infrastructure from a scalable perspective. The business values within the scope is to identify the touchpoints between the patients and the involved healthcare stakeholders in the telerehabilitation structure. The evaluation criteria concerns the system’s reliability, productivity and accessibility in terms of user experiences.

This entire project mainly went through 3 full iterative cycles.The first iteration aimed to explore the remote rehabilitation context and the potential end-user’s experiences. Here I adopted user research method to explore and evaluate the scenario data with medical domain experts (remote card sorting and co-creation), and data-enabled diary study approach to explore the collected data from the engaged participants.

For the second and third iterations, I focused on the (digital) product development. Within each iteration, I demonstrated design techniques like user research, user flow analysis, interative (functional) prototyping, iterative solution development and usability testing. Together with (hardware and software) developers, I experimented with the complex front-end and back-end systems to implement the final design in the context of iterative user evaluation.

At the end of the project, I presented the integration workflow with service blueprint to propose the way that could integrate the design system to the current cardiac telerehabilitation user flow and the platform infrastructure. The findings provide guidelines for designing interaction between user’s eating behaviors and home system performance, also demonstrated how user’s behavior data can be communicated between hardware and software.

Iterative cycles and solution development schema 

The first iteration focused on user research method to explore the context and design arguments. And the second & third iterations focused on the (digital) product development.

Card-sorting session to explore the current workflow with medical stakeholders 

The challenge was first identified through discussion with the medical industry clients. There are two major obstacles. First, in the context of cardiac rehabilitation, it is difficult for the clinical healthcare provider to keep track of the cardiac patient's condition while also meeting the patients' satisfaction. Second, as users, these patients struggle to maintain a motivated healthy lifestyle at home.

To gain a more in-depth understanding of their current workflow from the perspectives of the medical stakeholders, I organized a card-sorting session to map the information, data, and approach that I developed through secondary research and previous conducted interviews. The goal of this session was to first gain a better understanding of the ideal clinical scenario and how experts would recognize the context that emerging technology might be implemented in patients' remote scenarios, for instance, the reliability, the possibility for sustainability, and accessibility.

These insights were then translated into an initial digital design system flow, which was evaluated in an experience-based session.

Card-sorting session and experience-based evaluation session setup 

Diary Study to inquest the user's context 

To learn more from the end-user's perspetives, I then translated the experts' insights and arguments into key components to be explored with the target user groups to learn more about their experiences. One difficulty was that it was not possible to approach the real-patient group for research purposes. So I decided to recruit participants who shared similar goals and motivations, and then brought them to a similar environment that I built on the foundation of a real rehabilitation setting. Eventually, I evaluated with the user's experiences using the behavior mapping methods and semi-structure interviews.

Diary study setup and evluation criteria 

Co-creation session based on user research findings 

In previous iterations, questions generated during the initial discussion were decoded into elements that were evaluated through context enquiry study. As a result, I translated these user ideas into a co-creation session with medical experts to discuss the data from the user research. I first presented my findings through user journey mapping. The experts were then tasked with mapping the decoded the elements which they considered important to the target scenario, whereafter two types of evalutation criteria were used to evaluate with proposed direction and approaches. The arguments were thus encoded to drive the further design steps.

High-fidelity prototyping & Final iterative cycle  

Following the findings and insights from the second iteration, a series of different tests were conducted for the final prototype and its field implementation of the project. A thorough schema about how I have prototyped and decided can be accessed in the report, in which I analyzed the findings, detailing the elements and then synthesizing the investigated possibilities. After numerous technical experiments with software(java) and hardware(arduino) developments, the prototype and the implementation setting were determined.

Conceptual model accross the telerehabilitation domains

Service Design & Patient/User Journey  

A service blueprint is therefore built to visualize the patient/ user journey with the interactions among stakeholders and services relevant to the design. The patient is introduced to cardiac rehabilitation (CR), where he or she learns about the importance of maintaining personal health. Especially following a healthy eating pattern is emphasized. Therefore, specific diet goals are identified for the patient to follow during the CR program. Having regular fruit intake, for example, is one of the prioritized goals for the patient. The patient is introduced to the system at the start of the CR program. For first-time use, there will be a greeting message sent from an app to guide the user through the first set according to the patient’s identified goal, and then mapping the device. For everyday use, a series of looping behaviors is provided by an interactive fruit bowl to support the patient’s desired lifestyle, at the same time, collecting the patient’s performed behaviors. A connected application is used to evaluate the patient’s contextual situation, visualize the user’s performance in relation to the target goal, and even provide feedback and suggestions.
