A systematic deployment to support personal fruit-intake behaviors in the home-based cardiac rehabilitation context.

This is collaborated project with Maxima Medisch Centrum in the Netherlands in an e-health domain. The final deliverable of the project is an ioT system that includes a tangible design and the connected digital UI design. The goals are to involve e-health technology to improve the current-used digital platform for being seamlessly integrated with existing workflows and infrastructure from a scalable perspective. The business values within the scope is to identify the touchpoints between the patients and the involved healthcare stakeholders in the telerehabilitation structure. The evaluation criteria in terms of user's e-health experiences concerns the designed system’s informativeness, effectiveness, accessibility and explores the potential on user's sustained desired behaviors

This entire project mainly went through 4full iterative cycles.The first iteration aimed to explore the remote rehabilitation context and the potential end-user’s experiences. Here I adopted user research method to explore and evaluate the scenario data with medical domain experts (remote card sorting and co-creation), and data-enabled diary study approach to explore the collected data from the engaged participants.

For the second and third iterations, I focused on the (digital) product development. Within each iteration, I demonstrated design techniques like user research, user flow analysis, interative (functional) prototyping, iterative solution development and usability testing. Together with (hardware and software) developers, I experimented with the complex front-end and back-end systems to implement the final design in the context of iterative user evaluation.

In the final evaluation, I extended from the values of the focused design aspect to the broader cardiac-rehabilitation perspective, where I structured the UX flow(both front & back-end) and implemented the identified design values to the entire telerehabilitation context for the final evaluation. The findings provide guidelines for designing interaction between user’s eating behaviors and home system performance, also demonstrated how user’s behavior data can be communicated between hardware and software.

Time frame: 01.2022 - 05.2022
Client: Maxima Medisch Centrum
Techniques/Tools: User Research (card-sorting, workshop, diary study), Arduino(hardware development), figma(UI development), Rhino (CAD), 3D print, Usability Testing, Data Analysis

E-health Journey in the home context 

Iterative cycles and solution development schema 

Card-sorting session to explore the current workflow with medical stakeholders 

The challenge was first identified through discussion with the medical industry clients. There are two major obstacles. First, in the context of cardiac rehabilitation, it is difficult for the clinical healthcare provider to keep track of the cardiac patient's condition while also meeting the patients' satisfaction. Second, as users, these patients struggle to maintain a motivated healthy lifestyle at home.

To gain a more in-depth understanding of their current workflow from the perspectives of the medical stakeholders, I organized a card-sorting session to map the information, data, and approach that I developed through secondary research and previous conducted interviews. The goal of this session was to first gain a better understanding of the ideal clinical scenario and how experts would recognize the context that emerging technology might be implemented in patients' remote scenarios, for instance, the reliability, the possibility for sustainability, and accessibility.

These insights were then translated into an initial digital design system flow, which was evaluated in an experience-based session.

Card-sorting session and experience-based evaluation session setup 

Diary Study to inquest the user's context 

To learn more from the end-user's perspetives, I then translated the experts' insights and arguments into key components to be explored with the target user groups to learn more about their experiences. One difficulty was that it was not possible to approach the real-patient group for research purposes. So I decided to recruit participants who shared similar goals and motivations, and then brought them to a similar environment that I built on the foundation of a real rehabilitation setting. Eventually, I evaluated with the user's experiences using the behavior mapping methods and semi-structure interviews.

Diary study setup and evluation criteria 

Co-creation session based on user research findings 

In previous iterations, questions generated during the initial discussion were decoded into elements that were evaluated through context enquiry study. As a result, I translated these user ideas into a co-creation session with medical experts to discuss the data from the user research. I first presented my findings through user journey mapping. The experts were then tasked with mapping the decoded the elements which they considered important to the target scenario, whereafter two types of evalutation criteria were used to evaluate with proposed direction and approaches. The arguments were thus encoded to drive the further design steps.

Usability testing on the criteria 

5 participants who were also engaged in the previous home-deployment iteration were invited to continue evaluating on their experiences using digital UI. The goal of the study was to evaluate how users would recognize the connection between user data visualization, and the digital guidance through the telerehabbilitation journey concerning the accessibility, usefulness, and informativeness on the provided challenges. There were 5 challenges as shown in below. The criteria used are feasibility, accessibility, the effectiveness of the system design, and user satisfaction.

Challenge 1: To investigate how well users can select the specified telerehabilitation domain(s), individual goal(s), and recognizing the specified the sensing tools and services.

Challenge 2:To learn how the users would think of/take the automatic suggestions (database) based on personalized profile

Challenge 3:To understand how user can recognize and do the installation through digital guidance at home

Challenge 4:To explore how users can connect to the data shown on the dashboard for self-reflection, and the interactive ways with chatbot

Challenge 5:To explore how users would identify the integration with different telerehabilitation activities on the digital platform
